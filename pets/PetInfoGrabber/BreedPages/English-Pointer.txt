				<th colspan="2">
					Overview</th>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<td class="other-info" colspan="2">
					The Pointer, often called the English Pointer, is a medium to large-sized breed of dog developed as a gun dog. The Pointer is a large purebred known for being alert, courageous, energetic, gentle, independent, loyal, protective, and sweet. The most common colors for Pointers are black, brown, white, and yellow. To keep it healthy, it will need to get exercise regularly. Known to be multi-talented, the Pointer participates in a wide variety of activities such as hunting, pointing, and tracking. More so than many Sporting breeds, he has a protective nature and is an excellent watchdog. The Pointer is much more than a fine hunting dog or field trialer. He can become a devoted friend if you give him a chance.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<th colspan="2">
					Body type</th>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<td class="other-info" colspan="2">
					A properly proportioned English Pointer is roughly square in shape. The length of the front legs (measured from point of elbow to the ground) is slightly longer than the deepest part of the body.<br />
					<br />
					The Pointer has a shoulder height of 58-71 cm (23-28 in) and weighs 20-34 kg (45-75 lbs).  Most countries&#39; breed standards prefer symmetry and balance to perfect size, and most will allow an amount of variation if a dog&#39;s size does not encumber it in the field.  Pointers have a straight, tapered tail carried flat and breed characteristic oval feet. They have a compact, alert appearance.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<th colspan="2">
					Coat</th>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<td class="other-info" colspan="2">
					The coat is short, dense, close fitting, with a sheen. Possible colors are liver, lemon, black, or orange; Pointers can be solid colored or white with colored speckles (&lsquo;ticking&rsquo;) or larger markings. Dark colored Pointers have black or brown noses and light colored Pointers have light colored noses. They may also be any of the above as solid colors; the body of most Pointers is mainly white, but there may be some body markings.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<th colspan="2">
					Color</th>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<td class="other-info" colspan="2">
					Any of the following three coat colors, solid or in combination with white: black, liver, and any shade of yellow. Tri-color is also acceptable but is a hound trait and should be faulted accordingly. Dogs with yellow or yellow and white coats that have black noses and eye rims are traditionally referred to as &quot;orange colored.&quot; Yellow or yellow and white dogs with liver noses and eye rims are referred to as &quot;lemon colored.&quot;</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<th colspan="2">
					Temperament</th>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<td class="other-info" colspan="2">
					The Pointer looks like an aristocrat, and he is, but he&rsquo;s also fun-loving and mischievous from the get-go.  The Pointer gets along very well with other dogs and shows no aggression towards cats or other household pets. Pointers are great with children, but puppies may a bit too boisterous for small children. Pointers do not mind strangers, but their large size and deep bark may frighten those who are not comfortable with them.<br />
					<br />
					A Pointer isn&rsquo;t your everyday family dog, though. He&rsquo;s a highly engineered hunting machine, the Ferrari of the Sporting breeds. Plan to spend time each day giving him the exercise he needs. A couple of long runs, the opportunity to run alongside your bike and, his favorite, going hunting or training for hunting are all good ways to help him expend all his energy.</td>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<th colspan="2">
					Active / Watchdog Ability</th>
			</tr>
			<tr>
				<td class="other-info" colspan="2">

					<br />
					This dog will bark and alert its owners when an intruder is present. It is moderately protective of its owners and are generally considered to be good guardians.</td>
